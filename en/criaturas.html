Yōkai

Yōkai are supernatural creatures from Japanese folklore. They can be monsters, spirits or magical beings. Some are evil, others mischievous and some even help humans. They are very diverse and represent fears, ancient legends and inexplicable natural phenomena.

‹

Oni

Oni are demons or giant creatures, usually with red or blue skin, horns and brutal strength. They often appear as violent beings that punish the wicked or cause misfortune. In many stories they represent evil, but they can also be guardians or divine punishment.

‹

Kitsune

Kitsune are magical foxes with great intelligence and supernatural powers. It is said that the older they are, the more tails they have (up to nine). They can transform into humans, especially women. Some kitsune are protectors and messengers of the god Inari, while others trick people.

‹

Tengu

Tengu are beings that are half human and half bird, associated with mountains and forests. They are depicted with wings and, in some versions, with a red face and a long nose. They are expert warriors and are linked to samurais. They can be dangerous, but also protectors of nature.

‹

Yūrei

Yūrei are spirits of people who died with intense emotions such as sadness, hatred or revenge. They are believed to be unable to rest until they fulfill a purpose. They are depicted wearing white clothes, with long loose hair and a pale appearance. They are very common in Japanese horror stories.
