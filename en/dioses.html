Gods

Amaterasu (Goddess of the Sun) Susanoo (God of the Sea and Storms) Tsukuyomi (God of the Moon) Izanagi & Izanami (Creators of Japan)
‹

Amaterasu

Amaterasu Omikami is the goddess of the sun and one of the most important figures in Shintoism. She is considered the protector of Japan and, according to tradition, the Japanese imperial family is descended from her. She represents light, life, order and prosperity. Her most famous myth tells that she hid in a cave and the world was left in darkness.

‹

Susanoo

Susanoo no Mikoto is Amaterasu's brother and is known as the god of the sea, storms and chaos. He has an impulsive and destructive personality, but is also a hero in some legends. His most famous story is when he defeated the monster Yamata-no-Orochi (an eight-headed dragon) and found the legendary sword Kusanagi.

‹

Tsukuyomi

Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto is the god of the moon and Amaterasu's brother. He represents night, mystery and balance with the Sun. According to mythology, he separated from Amaterasu after a conflict, and that is why the sun and moon are never in the sky together at the same time.

‹

Izanagi & Izanami

Izanagi (male god) and Izanami (female goddess) are the creator gods of the world and the islands of Japan. According to the myth, they created the land by stirring the sea with a sacred spear. They also gave birth to other important gods. Izanami died giving birth to the god of fire, and her story is connected to the origin of death and the underworld in Japan.
