Japanese
Mythology

Japanese mythology is a world full of mystery, gods and supernatural creatures. Through Shintoism, Japan has built stories where nature is alive thanks to the kami, spirits that inhabit mountains, rivers and forests. In these legends gods like Amaterasu appear, beings like the yōkai and ancient tales that still inspire Japanese art and culture.

⌄

Shintoism

Shintoism is Japan's traditional religion and is based on spiritual connection with the natural world. It has no specific founder or single sacred book, but is transmitted through rituals, temples and ancient traditions.

⌄

Kami

Kami are spirits or deities present in Japanese mythology. They can represent forces of nature, ancestors or sacred beings. They are not always "gods" as in other religions, but energies that protect, guide or influence the world.

⌄

Relationship
with
Nature

Japanese mythology is deeply connected to nature, as it is believed that kami dwell in elements such as mountains, rivers, trees and animals. Therefore, nature is seen as something sacred, worthy of respect and admiration.
